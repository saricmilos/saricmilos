Weibull Distribution Analysis

The Weibull distribution shows the frequency of different wind speeds at this location. This information is crucial for estimating power production and selecting optimal turbine models.

Understanding the Weibull Distribution

The Weibull distribution is a probability function commonly used to model wind speed variations. It is defined by two key parameters:

  • Scale parameter (A): Relates to the average wind speed at the location
  • Shape parameter (k): Describes the wind speed variation - higher values indicate more consistent winds

This chart shows both the probability density (blue area) and the estimated hours per year (orange line) for each wind speed value. The analysis helps in determining the expected energy production and selecting the most suitable turbine for your specific location.

Technical Specifications

Power Curve

The power curve shows the relationship between wind speed (m/s) and power output (kW). Cut-in speed is the minimum wind speed at which the turbine begins producing usable power. Rated speed is when the turbine reaches its maximum power output. Cut-out speed is when the turbine shuts down to prevent damage from high winds.
